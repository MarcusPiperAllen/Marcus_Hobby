Who I Am

I'm Marcus, a web developer who likes working with people who have something real to share — a practice, a business, a mission — but don't have the time or energy to wrestle with their website. I help turn scattered notes, links, and ideas into a site that feels solid and easy to navigate.

What I Focus On

  • Turning messy content into clear pages. Most people have the content — they just need help organizing it into a structure that makes sense.
  • Showing before/after improvements. I believe in demonstrating transformation visually so clients can see the value.
  • Building simple tools that solve specific problems. Whether it's a quiz app, an SMS alert system, or a content organizer, I focus on practical solutions.
  • Working iteratively with clients. I keep people in the loop, break projects into manageable steps, and make sure the site matches how they actually work.
  • Long-term maintainability. I build sites that are easy to update and expand as needs grow.

How I Work

Every project starts with listening. I gather your content, understand your goals, and figure out what your visitors actually need to see.

Then I move into structure: pages, navigation, and priorities. What's the most important message? What do people need to find quickly? What can wait?

From there, I design and implement the site with a focus on clarity and long-term maintainability. I use AI tools to help with structure and speed, but every decision, layout, and final line of code is reviewed and owned by me.

The result is a site that clearly explains who you are, what you do, and how to work with you — without feeling like a generic template or an overwhelming project.

Beyond the Screen

Outside of work, I care a lot about health, family, and long-term projects. That mindset shows up in my development work — I'd rather build something steady and maintainable than chase quick hacks. I stay curious, follow good teachers, and use solid tools. If you're looking for someone who values clarity, structure, and real results, we'll probably work well together.
